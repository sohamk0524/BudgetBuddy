
# Did BudgetBuddy solve a problem?
Generally, this app did **not** solve a problem. It excited people and they said they saw the value, but they did not commit to logging their transactions. Without transaction data, the app is less personalized and the utility drops. This was the main driving factor in people dropping the app.
Moving forward, if we want this app to succeed we need an automated way to log transactions (either build trust in linking your bank account or find some other way). 

# Qualitative Insights
Overall, people liked the conceptual idea of the app, but did not commit to it. The main obstacle is people did not fully commit to logging all their transactions (not a single person logged *all* their transactions) and would not trust the app to link to their bank account. Note linking bank account is not a supported feature yet, we just asked if they would be willing to.
People loved the idea of deals. They wanted the deals to be more personalized and excite them but this is difficult without consistent transactions to base the recommendations off of.
The insights tab also excited people, but people were reluctant to take it seriously when it did not reflect their true financial data. Note that the insights tab did not seem like a significant feature for most people.
The overall user experience was very good. People noted the high latency of the recommendations but it was not a critical issue. As with milestone 2, people loved the simplicity of the app and it was clear what each tab was for.

# Quantitative Insights
- 8 people used the app for more than one day and generally opened the app around 4-6 times
- 2 people opened the app 1-2 times on the first day and did not use it after that
- People who logged transactions logged around 5-6 transactions
- Out of all transactions, most (~80%) were done manually
- ~15% of transactions logged were done with voice
- ~5% of transactions were done with receipt scanning
- People refreshed the tips page 4-5 times on average
- 8/10 people logged at least one transaction
- 0 people logged ALL of their transactions during the usage period
- 6 out of those 8 people stopped logging transactions after the second day of usage

**Note**: the two people who did not use the app after the first day were the people that did not log any transactions.

# Key Insights
Here are the top 1-2 points for each of the interview stages.

#### Pain
- People are only aware of overspending *after* it happens
- People wanted to spend less money on eating out with sacrificing on going out with friends

#### Promise
- They would want the app to magically keep track of all expenses and prevent overspending entirely
- They would want the app to save them a significant amount of money (especially on eating out)

#### Anxiety
- The deals were not as personalized as they wanted. The general sentiment was "Oh that's a cool deal, I might check it out sometime". They appreciated the deal but it did not excite them as much as we would've hoped

#### Inertia
- They forget to log in the moment, and then forget the transaction details or just deem it isn't worth it. The utility of the app goes down (deals & insights).
- The deals aren't as personalized without enough transaction data, which leads to people dropping the app.
